Q7: AIESEC Pakistan Content Hub: Submission & Tracking System

To streamline content submission and ensure brand consistency, I propose the **AIESEC Pakistan Content Hub**, built on accessible Google Workspace tools.

System Name:

AIESEC Pakistan Content Hub

Illustrative System Components:

  • Master Content Calendar (Google Sheet) - _e.g., docs.google.com/spreadsheets/..._
  • Content Submission Form (Google Form) - _e.g., forms.gle/..._
  • Content Asset Drive (Google Drive) - _e.g., drive.google.com/drive/..._
  • Brand Guidelines Checklist (Google Doc) - _e.g., docs.google.com/document/..._

How it Works:

üìÖ

Master Content Calendar

Central Google Sheet for all national and LC posting slots, minimizing clashes.

üìù

Content Submission Form

Standardized Google Form with content details and a mandatory brand guideline checklist.

üìÇ

Content Asset Drive

Dedicated Google Drive folder for LCs to upload high-resolution content assets.

The National Marketing team reviews submissions, updates status in the calendar, and automated notifications (e.g., via Zapier or Google Forms add-ons) inform LCs of their content's status. This minimizes compatibility issues and simplifies training for distributed teams.

How I would Introduce it to LCs:

1. Pilot Program & Feedback ▼

I would first introduce the system to 2-3 LCs as a pilot program. This allows us to identify any unforeseen issues, gather feedback, and refine the process before a wider launch, ensuring it's truly user-friendly for Pakistani LCs.

2. Comprehensive Training Session (Virtual) ▼

A mandatory virtual training session for all LC Marketing VPs and their relevant team members would cover: the benefits for LCs (clear slots, faster approvals), a live walkthrough of the system, and a deep dive into common brand guideline errors relevant to AIESEC in Pakistan's context.

3. Creation of a "How-To" Guide ▼

A simple, visually appealing Google Doc or PDF guide with screenshots and clear instructions would be created and shared widely for easy reference, adaptable for local nuances.

4. Dedicated Support Channel ▼

Establish a temporary WhatsApp group or a specific email address for LCs to ask questions and get immediate support during the initial rollout phase, considering popular communication methods in Pakistan.

5. Regular Feedback & Iteration ▼

Emphasize that this is a living system. Encourage ongoing feedback from LCs to continuously improve and adapt the process, fostering a sense of ownership and collaboration across Pakistan's diverse LCs.

6. Incentivize Compliance ▼

Potentially introduce a "Star LC" recognition for LCs consistently following guidelines and submitting on time, encouraging positive behavior and healthy competition nationwide.
